RPNow.net

RPNow is a web app for collaborative storytelling and writing. During its 4 year run, 32,000,000 posts have been sent between 120,000 unique users.

The technology stack behind RPNow has evolved over the years. Previously, it was a Single-Page App powered by Docker, Nginx, Postgres, Node, Express, Socket.IO, and Angular 5. Today, it is a self-hosted application that uses Vue and Golang, distributed as a single executable file.

Although the canonical instance at rpnow.net no longer exists, RPNow has since become an open-source, self-hostable project that anyone can download and deploy.

184

An experiment in bringing responsiveness and touch support to the look and feel of the Gameboy. You can explore this monochrome world on your computer or on your smartphone.

I posted the accompanying animation on Tumblr, and it briefly went viral, gaining over 1,400 likes and reblogs in one day.

184 was written in pure JavaScript on an HTML5 canvas.

Tone's Adventure

This is a homebrew Gameboy Color game written in C using the GBDK compiler. Yes, it works on a real Gameboy Color!

Although it was never finished, several demos are available to download. The easiest way to play them is by using a browser-based Gameboy emulator, such as GameBoy Online (in-browser)

gameboy-sound

gameboy-sound is an easy 3 kB JavaScript library for 98% accurate Gameboy audio in the browser!

It was created out of frustration with the overwhelming surface area of the web audio API, as well as with how big MP3 files are. I want the retro music in my games to come in retro file sizes!
